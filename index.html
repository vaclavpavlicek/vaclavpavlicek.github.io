About me

I am a third-year Electronic and Information Engineering student at Imperial College London interested in signal processing, algorithms, electronics and space exploration. Currently, I am undertaking a research placement with Dr Ayush Bhandari. The main goal of the placement is to develop algorithms that improve Unlimited Sampling Framework. In the summer of 2022, I interned at European Space Operations Centre in Darmstadt on a mission INTEGRAL. I developed a mission planning validation tool and also transponder swap scheduling to help to automate the operations processes. I published a paper about this work. I am open to any opportunites that align with my previously mentioned interests and I am looking for PhD opportunities.

Flight computer

I work on a rocket flight computer project with Imperial College Space Society colleagues. This flight computer will be placed and mounted in the rocket and will record telemetry from the rocket and it will transmit data from the rocket to the ground station.

GitHub Details

Human voice synthesiser

Within the group of two, we developed a human voice synthesiser in Simulink and MATLAB. The synthesiser was based on Linear Predictive Coding (LPC) - a method used during audio transmission (mobile phones or audio conferencing applications).

Details

INTEGRAL automation

During my 3-month summer internship at European Space Operations Centre, I developed two Python tools - a mission plan checker and a transponder swap scheduler. Both projects ensure that the safety limits of the satellite are not violated.

Paper Details

2nd-year Rover Project

My main responsibility was to work on the ESP32 control code which integrated different different subsystems together and was used to control the rover. I established communication between FPGA and ESP32 and WiFi communication between ESP32 and web server.

GitHub Details

Compiler project

As part of the Instruction Architecture and Compilers module, our task was to write C-to-MIPS Compiler in a group of two. The compiler was written in C++ with the use of Bison and YACC. Our compiler received the 5th highest score out of approximately 80 compilers.
