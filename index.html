About me

I am a third-year Electronic and Information Engineering student at Imperial College London interested in signal processing, algorithms, electronics and space exploration. Currently, I am undertaking a research placement with Dr Ayush Bhandari. The main goal of the placement is to develop algorithms that improve Unlimited Sampling Framework. In the summer of 2022, I interned at European Space Operations Centre in Darmstadt on a mission INTEGRAL. I developed a mission planning validation tool and also transponder swap scheduling to help to automate the operations processes. I published a paper about this work. I am open to any opportunites that align with my previously mentioned interests and I am looking for PhD opportunities.

Projects

Flight computer

I work on a rocket flight computer project with Imperial College Space Society colleagues. This flight computer will be placed and mounted in the rocket and will record telemetry from the rocket and it will transmit data from the rocket to the ground station. My responsibility involves developing the software for the computer and the project logistics.

Human voice synthesiser

Within the group of two, we developed a human voice synthesiser in Simulink and MATLAB. The synthesiser was based on Linear Predictive Coding (LPC) - a method used during audio transmission (mobile phones or audio conferencing applications). We used Simulink Coder to generate a C code and upload it to the microprocessor TI2000.

INTEGRAL automation

During my 3-month summer internship at European Space Operations Centre in Darmstadt (Germany), I developed two Python tools - a mission plan checker and a transponder swap scheduler. Both projects ensure that the safety limits of the satellite are not violated. The projects are in more detail described in a paper (link) published in the Journal of Student Research.

2nd-year Rover Project

During the 2nd year group project, I worked in a group of 6 on a rover. My main responsibility was to work on the ESP32 control code which integrated different different subsystems together and was used to control the rover. I established communication between FPGA and ESP32 which used the UART protocol to transmit vision information from the camera and WiFi communication.

Compiler project

As part of the Instruction Architecture and Compilers module, our task was to write C-to-MIPS Compiler in a group of two. The compiler was written in C++ with the use of Bison and YACC. Our compiler received the 5th highest score out of approximately 80 compilers and its source code can be found on GitHub.

Space society hackathon

Together with friends we participated in the Imperial College Space Society hackathon during which we propose a docking system for two spacecrafts. After brainstorming about a couple of ideas we settled on Iris capturing mechanism. I used Fusion 360 to create animation of docking of two spacecrafts that used our system.

Lumiroq

At the beginning of year 2, I participated in the NASA Space Apps Challenge together with other three colleagues from the Imperial College Space Society. Our project showed the importance of the light curves of asteroids in astronomy that help to estimate the size and shape of the asteroids. Our team was selected as one of the global nominees from other London teams.

1st-year CPU Project

Our team won the Head of Department’s Prize for the Best First Year Group Project. The project involved creating a CPU in a graphical editor ISSIE. I pipelined the CPU and then I also implemented two versions of the floating point unit (FPU) - one version optimized for latency and the other for throughput.

ESA Astro Pi

Our team Pardubice Pi was marked as one of the TOP 10 of all 523 international teams from the Astro Pi competition. We wrote a code to take images of Earth from the ISS and we used the obtained photos to identify the loss of vegetation. I was responsible for the mission design, data processing in Python and the development of a data visualisation tool in JavaScript.

ESA CanSat 2019

Our team represented Czech Republic at internation finals of the ESA CanSat competition 2019. My primary role within the CanSat team was software. I wrote the software for the satellite and ground station in C++. I also developed a ground station data dashboard in JavaScript that visualized real-time data from the flight.
